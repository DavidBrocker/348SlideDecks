Inferential Statistics

Applied to Independent Groups

  • Our goal is still to test whether a difference is likely due to chance

  • With independnet samples, chance variation now includes differences between individuals in both groups.

Group Variance

Within and Between

To compare the means we need to know:

  • Between group variance: how much the answers from the experimental group differ from the control group.

  • Within group variance: how much the answers of all participants differ from one another.

Independent Samples t-test Overview

Used when we compare two different groups on a continuous outcome.

  • Examples:

    • Coffee drinkers vs. decaf drinkers on happiness

    • Students with music vs. silence during study

    • Phone use vs. no phone use before bed

  • Goal:

    • Is the mean difference between groups larger than what we‚Äôd expect by chance?

T-tests

  • IV must be nominal and have only 2 categories

    • Using experimental versus control

    • Any two categories (bad/good)

  • DV must be continuous

    • Scale of 1 to 10
    • Count

Independent samples t-test:

Assumptions of Independent Samples t-test

  • Random sampling:

    • The data is randomly sampled from a population 

  • Independence:

    • Each group must be made up of independent observations.

  • Homogeneity of variance:

    • Variance should be equal across groups (tested with Levene‚Äôs Test)

Interpreting Levene‚Äôs Test

  • Levene‚Äôs Test tells us whether the assumption of equal variances holds.

    • If p > .05 ‚Üí use the top row** (‚ÄúEqual variances assumed‚Äù)

    • If p < .05 ‚Üí use the bottom row (‚ÄúEqual variances not assumed‚Äù)

  • Normality

    • Each group‚Äôs distribution should be approximately normal

Why Do We Care?

We need the Levene‚Äôs Test, because if the variance from the experimental group is not equal to the variance of the control group, we have to use a different formula for the pooled standard deviation.

  • A significant Levene‚Äôs Test means a significant difference in the variances.

Design Summary

50 undergraduate students from XYZ University were randomly assigned to one of two groups. One group received a small piece of chocolate (70% dark cocoa) to consume before completing a 10-minute concentration task. The other group received no chocolate.   • IV: Chocolate (Yes vs. No)

Summary: Independent Samples t-test

  • Compares two independent groups
  • Tests whether the difference in means is significant
  • Uses pooled variance as the denominator
  • Check assumptions: independence, normality, equal variance
  • Commonly used in experimental designs
